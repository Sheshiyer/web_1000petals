What Is Sound Healing?

Sound healing is a modality in which a facilitator uses all or specific aspects of music and its frequency grids— one can improve their emotional, psychological, spiritual, physical, social, mental, and superficial — to improve the health and well being. Sound healing therapy improves many facets of our life, including emotional and social development, cognitive and motor functioning; It also improves psychological and psychiatric health. Healing with sound happens by having the participant experiencing music by means of relaxing in a lying down position and listening with intent, and allowing the frequencies that induce positive brainwaves and alter our mood. Most of verything we experience in the universe is simply our perception of frequency or waves. When sound frequency reaches our ears, they are converted into electrical code that travels up via the auditory nerve into the auditory cortex. This is the part of the brain that processes sound.

Once sound wave reaches our brain, it triggers a series of responses in our bodies. This response alters our emotions, releases hormones and chemicals that affect both our bodies and our moods, and triggers an emotional impulse. There is some research based evidence to suggest that musicians have different brains than those who are not musically inclined. Research also has shown that the brains of musicians are more symmetrical and that the parts of the brain that are responsible for motor and cognitive functioning, coordination, reasoning, as well as the processing of information, are significantly larger. The two hemispheres of the brain have better communication thanks to an enlarged corpus callosum, as well.
